code of conduct

the short, plain-language version of how to be a decent person here. for the full legal text see privacy policy & terms of service, but if you just want the gist, read this instead.

the basics

be decent to other players
no harassment, threats, hate speech, or targeted abuse in messages, friend requests, or anywhere else in the game. disagreements happen, be an adult about it.
don't fake your stats
the leaderboard is a fun way to compare progress, not a competition worth cheating for. submitting fabricated or manipulated stats gets your entry removed.
keep profiles appropriate
your bio, username, avatar, and banner are public. nothing sexually explicit, no hate symbols, no illegal content, no extreme gore, no political content, and don't impersonate other players or real people.
respect other people's accounts
don't try to access accounts that aren't yours. don't share your own password or backup keys with anyone. we will never ask for either.
no spam
don't flood other players with friend requests or messages, and don't hammer the servers with automated requests. rate limits exist, we'd rather you didn't test them.
tinker with your own stuff, leave the server alone
the client-side code is open-source and yours to mess with. your local save is your data, edit it however you like. just don't attack, exploit, or poke at the servers themselves.

edge cases people actually ask about

alt accounts
having more than one account isn't against the rules on its own. using alts to fake leaderboard rankings, farm friend request limits, evade a suspension, or get around a removed leaderboard entry is. if we catch it, all the accounts involved get action taken, not just the newest one.
save editing vs. account cheating
editing your own local save with dev tools, a hex editor, or whatever else is fine, that data is yours and it never leaves your browser unless you sync it. the line is the leaderboard: if an edited save gets synced and then submits stats publicly, that counts as fabricated stats, and the entry gets pulled the same as any other cheat.
bots, scripts, and automation
automating your own client to play while you're away (auto-clickers, idle scripts) doesn't touch our servers any differently than normal play, so it's not something we police. automation that hits the backup, leaderboard, or messaging apis directly, outside of the normal client, is not allowed, since that's what the rate limits exist to catch. ONLY use in-game features like auto roll and ways to roll faster via in-game stuff, not external scripts and panels that allow you to bypass that (or any kind of bypass, in fact!).
minors on the platform
the game itself is general-audience. accounts and social features are not intended for children under 13. if a report or evidence suggests an account belongs to a child under 13, we'll disable social features on it and, where an email is on file, reach out. this isn't punitive, it's just not a space we can knowingly host that data in.
off-platform harassment
if a dispute that started in-game (leaderboard beef, a friend falling out, whatever) spills into harassment on discord, github, or elsewhere, and it's reported with evidence, we can still act on the in-game account involved. we can't moderate other platforms, but we're not going to pretend the harassment didn't originate here either.
doxxing and real-world info
posting someone else's real name, address, workplace, or other identifying info without consent, anywhere in the game (bio, messages, leaderboard username), is an immediate removal of the content and likely account action. this applies even if the info is technically public elsewhere.
self-harm or crisis content in messages
if you see another player express something that sounds like they're in crisis, we'd rather you reach out kindly or point them to a hotline than report it as a conduct issue. this isn't a conduct violation, it's just not something automated moderation is equipped to triage well, so a human report with context helps more than a flag.
username and profile edge cases
leaderboard usernames are permanent, so we're stricter about what's allowed there than elsewhere:
  • slurs, hate symbols, or coded references to them: rejected outright, no appeal
  • impersonating a real person or another player: removed, account reviewed
  • edgy, dark, or crude humor that isn't targeting a group or person: allowed, this is a judgment call and we'd rather be permissive than paternalistic
  • usernames that look like official accounts ("admin", "authsrng-staff", etc): reserved, contact us if you want an exception
buying, selling, or trading accounts
there's nothing monetized in the game, so we don't expect this to come up, but account transfers outside of our systems aren't supported. if you hand your login to someone else, you're accepting whatever they do with it as your own responsibility, and we won't reverse actions taken on the account afterward on the basis that "it wasn't me."
reporting mistakes and false reports
reporting something that turns out to be fine isn't punished, we'd rather get reports we have to dismiss than miss real ones. repeatedly reporting the same player in bad faith, as a form of harassment on its own, is treated as harassment.
disputing an action taken against you
if your leaderboard entry, message, profile content, or account gets actioned and you think it was a mistake, email us with your username and what happened. we're one person running this, not a moderation team, so response times vary, but every appeal gets read.

enforcement

breaking these gets your stuff removed
depending on severity, that might mean a removed leaderboard entry, a deleted message or profile, or a suspended account. nothing personal, just keeping the place livable.
severity matters
a first-time crude username gets a quiet reset request. targeted harassment, doxxing, or account access violations get immediate and permanent action, no warning owed. we use judgment here rather than a fixed strike system, since a rigid point system tends to punish honest mistakes the same as bad-faith abuse.

this page is a friendly summary, not a legal document! the privacy policy & terms of service are what actually governs the game.
